About Me

I am Daniel Bruzual, computer engineer, entrepreneur and lover of the great outdoors. I was born in Venezuela but I have lived in several countries ever since I was young. I am currently based in Helsinki, Finland.

I hold a dual degree in MSc Service Design and Engineering from Aalto University and University of Trento (GPA 4.91/5.00). My technical specialization focuses on mobile and cloud systems. My minor is on Innovation and Entrepreneurship. I received my BSc Computer Engineering from Universidad Simón Bolívar in Venezuela (GPA 4.58/5.00).

I have a passion for mobile applications and have made several Android apps: for fun (Voz Del Pueblo), for contests (Together App), and for things I needed which didn't exist (ProRemote). More recently I have been involved with Docker and the Internet of Things. I have been studying the internals of Docker components, and wrote my thesis on this subject (Distributed Computing Framework for Heterogeneous IoT Devices). I even presented this work at DockerCon Europe 2017.

I am self-motivated and an autodidact. I taught myself how to make websites when I was 11 by getting up at 6:00 am on weekends and reading every guide I could find. When I was 16, I learnt how to program web applications in order to make a student survey system for my high school. They saved hundreds of hours of transcription every year, and were able to give teachers timely reports. I learn whatever skills I need to achieve my goals. For this reason, I do not like to limit myself to a set of technologies or tools.

I am currently working as a Research Assistant at Aalto University. I research about Fog computing on heterogeneous IoT devices. I am also a teaching assistant developing automated grading systems based on unit tests. Read more about what I am doing now here.

Before initiating my studies, I worked as a freelance developer making custom Wordpress themes and plugins. I also worked for 1 year at MobileMediaNetworks in Venezuela, where I developed mobile applications and backends for Augmented Reality in magazines. I also did a 5-month internship at Plista GmbH in Berlin, where I was developed several recommendation algorithms.

I am a polyglot, speaking Spanish, English, German, Italian and Portuguese fluently.

On my spare time you will find me on a mountain, or making videos, or probably both. I like trailrunning and mountain-hiking. I also have a passion for kayak, having recently completed a 5-day trip around the Aeolian Islands in Sicily. For the past two years I have been learning to make videos, and one day I aspire to submit one to a mountain film festival.
